Part A: Case Study – UPI QR Code Fraud
What it is: UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud involves tricking users into sending money or giving access to their bank accounts through digital payment apps. A common version is "QR Code Scam."

How it happens: 1. The fraudster poses as a buyer on a platform like OLX or Facebook Marketplace.
2. They claim they want to pay you and send a QR code via WhatsApp.
3. They tell you to "Scan this code to receive the payment."
4. When you scan it and enter your UPI PIN, money is actually debited from your account instead of being credited.

Who is targeted: Generally, people selling items online or those not familiar with the technicality that "Scanning a QR is only for paying, not receiving."

Consequences: Immediate financial loss, potential compromise of bank account links, and emotional distress.

Part B: Prevention Checklist (8 Essential Tips)
1.Never enter a UPI PIN to receive money: Remember, your PIN is only required for sending money.

2.Verify the recipient's name: Before clicking 'Pay' on any UPI app, double-check the name shown in the app.

3.Do not scan random QR codes: Only scan codes at trusted merchant shops or known websites.

4.Enable App Lock: Keep your UPI apps (GPay, PhonePe, Paytm) protected with a biometric or separate pattern lock.

5.Be wary of 'Request Money' notifications: Fraudsters often send payment requests; decline any request from an unknown person.

6.Use a strong UPI PIN: Avoid using simple codes like 1234 or your birth year.

7.Report immediately on 1930: If you are a victim of financial fraud, call the National Cybercrime Helpline at 1930.

8.Use the official portal: Report any cyber incidents at cybercrime.gov.in.
