Part A: Detailed Case Study
Definition and Overview:
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of digital financial crime where scammers deceive users into authorizing unauthorized transactions. One of the most prevalent methods used today is the "QR Code Scam." This exploit relies on the victim's misunderstanding of how QR technology works—specifically the fact that a QR code is only used to send money, never to receive it.

The Mechanism of the Fraud:
The scam typically begins on secondary marketplaces like OLX or Facebook Marketplace. The fraudster contacts a seller and expresses urgent interest in buying an item. To "pay" the seller, the scammer sends a QR code via WhatsApp, claiming it is a "Payment Received" voucher. They instruct the victim to scan the code and enter their UPI PIN to "release the funds" into their bank account. Once the victim enters the PIN, the transaction is authorized in reverse; instead of receiving money, the victim's account is instantly debited.

Target Audience and Impact:
The primary targets are individuals selling used goods online, students, and senior citizens who may not be tech-savvy. The consequences are often severe, ranging from the immediate loss of significant savings to the potential compromise of linked bank accounts. Beyond financial loss, victims often experience deep emotional distress and a loss of trust in digital payment systems.
