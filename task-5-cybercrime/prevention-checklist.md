Part B: Prevention Checklist (8 Essential Tips)
1.PIN is for Sending Only: Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Credits to your account happen automatically without a PIN.

2.Verify Identity: Always verify the name of the person you are interacting with before clicking any link or scanning a code.

3.Ignore Random QR Codes: Do not scan QR codes sent by strangers over WhatsApp or SMS, as these are almost always phishing attempts.

4.Secure Your Apps: Use biometric locks (fingerprint or face ID) and a strong, unique PIN for all payment apps like GPay or PhonePe.

5.Decline Unknown Requests: If you receive a "Collect Request" on your UPI app from an unknown ID, decline and block it immediately.

6.Limit Transaction Amounts: Set a daily limit on your UPI transactions through your bank's mobile app to minimize potential losses.

7.Official Reporting (1930): In case of fraud, immediately call the National Cybercrime Helpline at 1930 to freeze the transaction.

8.Use the National Portal: Report all digital crimes formally at cybercrime.gov.in to help authorities track the scammers.
