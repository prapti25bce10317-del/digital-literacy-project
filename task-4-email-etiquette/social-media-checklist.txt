5 Do’s
1.Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Always use an extra layer of security like an OTP or authenticator app to protect your accounts from hacking.

2.Verify Information Before Sharing: Always fact-check news or links to ensure you aren't spreading misinformation or "fake news."

3.Maintain a Professional Image: Remember that future employers often check social media; ensure your posts reflect your character positively.

4.Review Privacy Settings Regularly: Periodically check who can see your posts, tagged photos, and personal contact information.

5.Be Respectful in Online Discussions: Use polite language even when disagreeing with others to maintain a healthy digital environment.

5 Don’ts
1.Don’t Share Sensitive Personal Data: Never post your home address, phone number, Aadhaar details, or OTPs on any public platform.

2.Don’t Click on Suspicious Links: Avoid clicking links from unknown senders or "too good to be true" offers, as these are often phishing attempts.

3.Don’t Post Private Documents: Never share pictures of your college ID cards, exam hall tickets, or passwords.

4.Don’t Engage with Cyberbullies or Trolls: Avoid getting into heated public arguments; instead, block or report accounts that are being abusive.

5.Don’t Accept Every Friend Request: Only connect with people you know personally or have a verified professional reason to follow.
